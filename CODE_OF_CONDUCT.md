# Code of Conduct

> For the Turkish version of this document, see [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.tr.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.tr.md).

## Our Pledge

As members, contributors, and maintainers of the FlowSharp community, we pledge
to make participation in our project an open, welcoming, and respectful
experience for everyone. We are committed to providing a friendly environment
regardless of age, body size, level of experience, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or similar personal characteristics, free from
harassment and discrimination.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment:

- Being kind and respectful to other people
- Being open to differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing for our mistakes, and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community, not just for ourselves

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassing, demeaning, or offensive comments and conduct
- Personal or political attacks, and trolling
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address)
  without explicit permission
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable
behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any unacceptable behavior. Maintainers have the right to remove,
edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that
do not align with this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban
any contributor when warranted.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an
individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of harassing or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the
project team at **me@fatihoz.tr**. All complaints will be reviewed and
investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and
appropriate to the circumstances. The project team is obligated to maintain
confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.
